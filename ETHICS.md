# Ethics & responsible-use notes

## Review and consent
- The interview component is human-subjects research conducted under a protocol reviewed and
  approved by an independent Institutional Review Board prior to data collection.
- All interview participants provided informed consent; participants reviewed their own
  quotations before publication; politically exposed participants received additional
  protections appropriate to their risk.
- One participant withdrew consent to be named after reviewing their quotations; that consent
  is revocable and was honored in full (the participant is pseudonymized throughout).

## What is withheld, and why
- **Unmasked scoring file** — withheld to limit potential harm. Access conditions are described
  in the datasheet / available on approved request.
- **Interview transcripts** — not released; participant consent and safety preclude it.

## Corpus handling
- The computational corpus comprises **public** social-media posts and does not constitute
  human-subjects research. It was anonymized at intake: usernames and identifying details
  removed, **explicit slurs masked**, and post dates randomized within the collection window.
- Public figures named in political contexts are retained as matters of public record.

## Responsible use
- Do not attempt to deanonymize users or reconstruct removed identifiers.
- Do not use these materials to build or deploy production moderation systems as-is, or to
  generate harmful content.
- The corpus is language-level and purposively sampled; it is **not** representative of
  platform-wide base rates.
